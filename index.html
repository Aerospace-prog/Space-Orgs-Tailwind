Space Organisations

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), India's national space agency, headquartered in Bengaluru, is responsible for developing space technology and its applications for national development, space science research, and planetary exploration.

NASA, which stands for the National Aeronautics and Space Administration, is a United States government agency responsible for science and technology related to air and space, including space exploration and aeronautics research.

JAXA, which stands for the Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency, is Japan's national space agency, formed in 2003 through the merger of three organizations, and focuses on space exploration, research, and development.

The European Space Agency (ESA) is an intergovernmental organization that serves as Europe's gateway to space, aiming to shape the development of Europe's space capabilities and ensure space investments benefit citizens worldwide.
